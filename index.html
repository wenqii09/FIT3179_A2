Global Crime Rate (2003 - 2011)

This page demonstrates Crime Rate around the world from 2003 to 2011. "In ordinary language, a crime is an unlawful act punishable by a state or other authority. The term crime does not, in modern criminal law, have any simple and universally accepted definition, though statutory definitions have been provided for certain purposes. The most popular view is that crime is a category created by law; in other words, something is a crime if declared as such by the relevant and applicable law. One proposed definition is that a crime or offence (or criminal offence) is an act harmful not only to some individual but also to a community, society, or the state ("a public wrong"). Such acts are forbidden and punishable by law."

- (Wikipedia, 2020)

Line Chart

Line chart shows the total crime cases over the world from 2003 to 2011. We can see that overall crime cases has been increasing and only has started to have a more obvious decreasing trend from 2010 to 2011.

Map and Bar Chart

Below shows a map and bar chart on the crime rate per 10,000 population of different countries. Bar chart is created to show the difference in rank of the crime rate of the countries. From the map, we can see that Sweden has the highest crime rate compared to other countries from 2003-2011. Note that there are missing data for some countries due to data limitations.

There is a filter option for reader to explore on the minimum crime rate of different countries along with countries filter. Please note that only some popular countries are able to be filtered using the country selection filter option.

As it may be difficult to tell Sweden has the highest crime rate on the map, a bar chart is created to ease reader to find out the country with the highest crime rate which is Sweden. There is also be a filter option at the bottom of the bar chart for reader to explore more on crime rate per 10,000 population by Continent.

Interactive Bar chart (Type of Crime)

Stacked Bar Chart

Stacked bar chart on the right shows the age and gender of the victim. We can see that age group 16-24 has the most victim among all age group followed by 25-44, 45-64 and 65-84 by comparing the total percentage.
