Global Crime Rate (2003 - 2011)

Introduction

This page demonstrates Crime Rate around the world from 2003 to 2011. "In ordinary language, a crime is an unlawful act punishable by a state or other authority. The term crime does not, in modern criminal law, have any simple and universally accepted definition, though statutory definitions have been provided for certain purposes. The most popular view is that crime is a category created by law; in other words, something is a crime if declared as such by the relevant and applicable law. One proposed definition is that a crime or offence (or criminal offence) is an act harmful not only to some individual but also to a community, society, or the state ("a public wrong"). Such acts are forbidden and punishable by law."

- (Wikipedia, 2020)



Trend of Crime Rate

Line chart shows the total crime cases over the world from 2003 to 2011. We can see that the overall trend of total crime cases increased. From 2003 to 2007, there is an obvious increasing trend, followed by a plateau over 2007 to 2010. Then, it decreases from 2010 to 2011.

Note that the total cases depending on the data collected from Data World (2020). There are some missing data for some countries due to data limitations. Therefore, total crime cases varies with countries.


If you want to know the total crime cases specifically in each year, tooltip is available.

Comparing Crime Rate among Countries

Below shows a map and bar chart on the crime rate per 10,000 population of different countries. Bar chart is created to show the difference in rank of the crime rate of the countries. From the map, we can see that Sweden has the highest crime rate compared to other countries from 2003-2011. Note that there are missing data for some countries due to data limitations. The bar chart is inspired by Ali (n.d.).

There is a filter option (scroller) for reader to explore on the minimum crime rate of different countries along with countries filter. Please note that only some popular countries are able to be filtered using the country selection filter option. The name of the countries can be referred from the map.

As it may be difficult to tell Sweden has the highest crime rate on the map, a bar chart is created to ease reader to find out the country with the highest crime rate which is Sweden. There is also be a filter option at the bottom of the bar chart for reader to explore more on crime rate per 10,000 population by Continent.


Crime Type committed in Sweden

The top bar chart on the right shows the type of crime committed in Sweden over years. The bottom bar chart shows the total number of crimes committed for each category or type over years.


From the bar chart, we can see that 2003 has the highest total number of cases in Sweden compared to the rest. In terms of crime type (second bar chart), the category "stealing" has the highest total count compared to the rest of the category.


This is visualisation is inspired by Plotly (n.d.).








If you would like to explore the data further, click on the the category (crime type) you would like to explore of the bar chart below to see the how each crime changes over years. You will be able to explore the total crime committed for each category over years.

Age & Gender of Victims in Sweden

Stacked bar chart on the right shows the age and gender of the victim. We can see that age group 16-24 has the most victim among all age group, followed by 25-44, 45-64 and 65-84 by comparing the total percentage.


Both graph and data are inspired and retrieved from Brå (2020) respectively. Looking into all 4 different age group, surprisingly, male has the more victims compared to female in Sweden from 2003 to 2011.
